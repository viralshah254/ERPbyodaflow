# OdaFlow ERP – Product Overview

## 1. What OdaFlow ERP Is

OdaFlow is a **modern, mid‑market ERP for manufacturers, distributors, and multi‑store retailers**, focused on:

- **Inventory & Warehousing** – multi‑warehouse stock, locations, transfers, batch/lot tracking, reorder levels.
- **Products & Pricing** – rich product master with variants, packaging, UOMs, and flexible price lists.
- **Sales & Distribution** – quotes, orders, deliveries, invoices, customer/route management.
- **Purchasing & AP** – purchase requests, orders, receipts/GRNs, purchase returns, AP invoices/bills.
- **Manufacturing** – BOMs (formulas), routings, production orders, consumption, and yield.
- **Finance & Treasury** – basic GL integration, payment runs, cash collections.
- **Automation & Insights** – workflows, rules, dashboards, and **AI Tower** for assisted decisions.

The current codebase is **Next.js/React (app router)** with a **mocked backend layer** and **API specs in `docs/`** to guide real backend implementation.

For full details, see:

### 2.1 Products & Pricing

- **Product** (`ProductRow`)
  - Fields: `id`, `sku`, `name`, `category`, `status`, `baseUom`, `unit`, `baseBarcode`, `defaultSize`, etc.
  - Each product is the **master record** used across inventory, pricing, and manufacturing.

- **Product Variant**
  - Represents a *concrete sellable unit* (e.g. `SKU‑001‑1KG`, `SKU‑001‑500G`).
  - Attributes: size, grade, packaging type, other attribute pairs (e.g. `{"size": "1kg"}`).

- **Product Packaging**
  - Mapping between base unit and packs (e.g. `1 CASE = 12 EA`).
  - Used for pricing tiers and UOM‑aware inventory moves.

- **Price Lists**
  - Per channel/currency (Retail, Wholesale, Distributor, Export, etc.).
  - Product prices are stored per `priceListId` and variant/UOM.

### 2.2 Parties (Customers, Suppliers, Franchisees)

- Single **Party** model with:
  - `roles`: customer, supplier, franchisee, etc.
  - `customerType`: retailer, wholesaler, distributor, franchisee, etc.
  - `supplierType`: raw material, packaging, service, etc.
- Navigation layers filter this same model into:
  - `/master/parties`, `/sales/customers`, `/ap/suppliers`, franchise views, etc.

### 2.3 Inventory & Stock

- **StockLevel**
  - Dimensions: `productId`, `variantId`, `warehouseId`, `locationId`, `batchId`.
  - Quantities: `quantity`, `reserved`, `available`, `reorderLevel`, `status` (`IN_STOCK`, `LOW_STOCK`, `OUT_OF_STOCK`).

- **StockMovement**
  - Immutable movements (in/out) generated by:
    - Sales deliveries/invoices.
    - Purchase receipts/returns.
    - Production (consumption + output).
    - Stock adjustments, transfers.

- **StockAdjustment**
  - Explicit increase/decrease with reason, linked back to `StockLevel` + audit trail.

### 2.4 Manufacturing

- **BOMs (Formulas)**
  - Components with UOM, scrap %, optional flags.
- **Routings & Operations**
  - Sequence of operations, work centers, setup/run timings.

## 3. Frontend Architecture

- **Framework:** Next.js (App Router) + TypeScript.
- **State:**
  - Global: `zustand` stores (e.g. `auth-store`, `copilot-store`, `orgContextStore`).
  - Local: React state/hooks per page.
- **Data access (mock layer):**
  - `src/lib/data/*.repo.ts` – CRUD‑style functions backed by localStorage + mock data from `src/lib/mock`.
  - Stubbed API client at `src/lib/api/client.ts` and `src/lib/api/*` to prepare for real backend.

### 3.1 UI Building Blocks

- `PageShell`, `PageLayout`, `PageHeader` – standard page chrome, breadcrumbs, right panel.
- `DataTable`, `RowActions`, `FiltersBar`, `StatusBadge`, `Badge` – list/table UX.
- `EntityDrawer`, `Sheet` – create/edit flows (e.g. Add Product, stock adjustment).
- `Tabs`, `Select`, `Input`, `Card`, `Button` – Shadcn‑style primitives.

## 4. Key Flows (Current UX)

### 4.1 Product Master

- **Path:** `/master/products`
- **Features:**
  - List of products, search by name/SKU.
  - Add Product drawer capturing SKU, name, category, unit/UOM, base barcode, default size.
  - On create, user is redirected into **packaging/variants configuration**.
  - Row actions: view, edit, duplicate, delete (delete guarded by admin permission).

### 4.2 Inventory → Products & Stock Levels

- **Inventory → Products (`/inventory/products`)**
  - Shows products with **variant count** and **packaging count** badges.
  - Row click navigates to product detail.
  - Duplicate + delete wired via repository and stub API.

- **Inventory → Stock Levels (`/inventory/stock-levels`)**
  - Per‑warehouse/location stock list with **On hand / Reserved / Available / Status**.
  - **Stock Adjustment** sheet:
    - Increase/Decrease toggle.
    - Validated quantity input (supports empty, disables invalid).
    - Updates `StockLevel` state and status (`In stock`, `Low stock`, `Out of stock`).
  - **Transfer** action goes to `/warehouse/transfers` (alias also exposed under Inventory → Transfers).

## 5. Permissions & Security

- **User model** includes `roleIds`.
- **Client‑side permission helper:** `src/lib/permissions.ts`
  - `can(user, permission)` – placeholder for future policy engine.
  - `Permissions` – strings like `inventory.read`, `inventory.write`, etc.
  - `canDeleteEntity(user)` – **only admin roles** (currently `role-admin`) can delete *anything*.
- Delete buttons/actions are hidden for non‑admin users across:
  - Products, variants, BOMs, routings, price lists, UOMs, etc.
- **Backend MUST enforce the same rules** when real APIs are wired (see API specs).

## 6. AI Tower (Previously “Copilot”)

### 6.1 Concept

**AI Tower** is the in‑product AI assistant that helps users:

- Explain what they’re seeing (“Explain this pricing rule”, “Why is stock low here?”).
- Generate drafts (documents, workflows, pricing suggestions).
- Recommend actions across modules (e.g. create purchase orders for low stock).

Under the hood it is implemented via:

- `src/stores/copilot-store.ts` – drawer state and actions.
- `src/components/copilot/*` – `AI Tower` drawer, chat UI, action review, suggestion cards.
- `src/config/command-palette.ts` – command palette entries that open AI Tower or prefill prompts.

### 6.2 Naming Conventions to Use

When continuing development, **use “AI Tower” in all user‑facing UI**:

- Header button tooltip/title: “Open AI Tower”.
- Drawer title: “AI Tower”.
- Buttons: “Ask AI Tower”, “Generate with AI Tower”, “AI Tower suggestions”.
- Tooltips like `ExplainThis`: “Explain this. Opens AI Tower.”
- Command palette group and items:
  - Group label: “AI Tower”.
  - Items: “Ask AI Tower about this page”, “Open AI Tower”.

Internally you can keep store and type names (`copilot-store`, `CopilotAction`, etc.) for now; only the **labels and text** must read “AI Tower”.

## 7. Shortcuts & Entry Points for AI Tower

### 7.1 Global Keyboard Shortcut

- **⌘K / Ctrl+K** – opens the **command palette**, which includes AI Tower commands:
  - “Ask AI Tower about this page”.
  - “Open AI Tower”.
- Implementation:
  - `src/components/command/CommandPalette.tsx`
  - `src/config/command-palette.ts` (`COMMAND_COPILOT_ITEMS` – rename labels/group to AI Tower).

### 7.2 Click Entry Points

- **Header AI Tower button**
  - Top‑right icon (Sparkles) opens AI Tower drawer via `useCopilotStore().openDrawer()`.

- **Contextual “Explain this” icons**
  - Component: `src/components/copilot/ExplainThis.tsx`.
  - Small `?` icon near labels/fields.
  - On click: calls `openDrawerWithPrompt(prompt)` with a prefilled question about that context.

- **Ask AI Tower buttons on key pages**
  - Many overview/detail pages have “Ask Copilot” buttons wired to `openDrawerWithPrompt`.
  - When renaming, update button labels to “Ask AI Tower” but keep behavior identical.

- **AI Insights page**
  - `src/app/(dashboard)/automation/ai-insights/page.tsx` shows **AI Tower action cards** powered by mocks and opens AI Tower with specific actions.

## 8. How to Continue

For your dev team:

1. **Backend implementation**
   - Use the API contracts in:
     - `docs/BACKEND_PRODUCTS_API.md`
     - `docs/BACKEND_INVENTORY_STOCK_API.md`
     - `docs/BACKEND_PARTIES_API.md`
   - Implement real services and plug them into `src/lib/api/client.ts` and the `*.repo.ts` repositories.

2. **Auth & Permissions**
   - Replace the mock auth initialization with real login + JWT/session.
   - Implement `can()` in `src/lib/permissions.ts` backed by real role/permission data.
   - Ensure **delete endpoints** require `admin.delete`/admin role.

3. **AI Tower**
   - Swap out the current stubbed AI plumbing for your chosen LLM backend.
   - Extend `command-palette` AI Tower entries with more context‑aware prompts (per module).
   - Keep **⌘K** as the central shortcut for **AI Tower + navigation**.

4. **Rename Copilot → AI Tower in UI**
   - Grep for “Copilot” in `src/` and update visible text to “AI Tower” as per section 6.2.

This document should give enough context for any engineer to understand what OdaFlow ERP is, where to hook in real backend logic, and how AI Tower fits into the overall experience.
